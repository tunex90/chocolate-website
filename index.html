What is Chocolate?

Chocolate is a food made from roasted and ground cacao seed kernels. It is available as a liquid, solid, or paste, and is used in flavourings for other foods. The cacao tree has been used as far back as 1500 BC by the Olmec civilisation of ancient Mexico.

Today, chocolate is one of the most popular foods in the world, enjoyed by billions of people across every continent.

Fun Facts

  • It takes approximately 400 cocoa beans to make one pound of chocolate.
  • The word "chocolate" comes from the Aztec word xocolatl, meaning "bitter water".
  • Dark chocolate contains antioxidants that may benefit heart health.
  • Switzerland consumes the most chocolate per capita — about 10 kg per person per year.
  • White chocolate contains no cocoa solids — only cocoa butter.
